Story — From Alarm to Extraction

Jan 2001 — the alarm sounded and the crew snapped to. Sgt. Jackson told me to jump on the flight immediately. The RTO fed us the details: a family of hikers near Mount Whitney; the father had fallen off a ledge and broken his leg. I grabbed my log book, flight bag, and gear; pre‑flight checks were already done. We lifted with a four‑person crew — pilot, co‑pilot, medic (left door), and me as crew‑chief on the right. I rehearsed the hoist in my head the entire transit. It wasn’t long before we had the scene in sight. We orbited, confirmed winds, set LZ parameters, and brought the bird to a steady hover. I transferred the medic’s hoist, checked harness and comms, and called the drop. The winch sang. The medic swung down into spindrift and rock shadow. Minutes dragged. Then: “Package ready.” We brought the patient up clean. The cabin filled with cold and relief. We turned for home — one more life above the snow.
